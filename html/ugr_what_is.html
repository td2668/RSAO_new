What Is Research?

With Mount Royal's transition to a University, a wide breadth of research opportunities exist for our talented students to discover and take advantage of during their time here. There are a number of unique opportunities for students to participate in research and make a meaningful contribution to the academia of different disciplines.

Research encompasses collaboration between students and faculty, aimed at either the discovery of new knowledge or building off the foundation of existing knowledge. We want our students to embark on research that is both relevant to their studies and future career paths -- we want them to have the best experience possible.

Why Do Research?

Top Reasons to do Research:

  • To gain appreciation for the practical applications of knowledge, and to step outside your classroom and learn about the theories, tools, resources, and ethical issues that scholars and professionals encounter on a daily basis
  • You'll learn how to formulate questions, design plans to find answers, collect and analyze data, draw conclusions from that data, and share your findings with a community
  • It gives you an opportunity to connect with faculty at MRU on projects they are passionate about
  • Prepare for graduate school and the workplace, where they're looking for outstanding students who can identify and solve problems
  • Evaluate the information presented to you as a citizen of our complex society, and develop skills that will help to make informed decisions about all kinds of public policy issues that affect your everyday life
  • Develop independent thinking and creativity, time-management and budget skills, and confidence in your academic and career goals
  • Excellent work experience for resume
  • Network with people in your field of interest or expertise
  • You will learn to apply what you already know and have learned to new issues
  • Use this experience to help chose a future career path
  • It's fun, interesting and rewarding

Present Your Research

Students are able to present their research through a number of different oral and written submissions.

One of the most popular forms of presenting a research initiative is through visuals, such as posters that are displayed around campus. Essays, papers and reports are other examples of written ways to present your research.

Presenting your research orally comes at forums and conferences. In the past, Mount Royal has had students present their research at conferences both locally and across the country.

Why Get Involved in Research?

When you think of research, what's the first thing that comes to mind?

For many, its science and the discovery of hard, concrete facts but in reality, it involves subjects that span across language and culture, the humanities, arts and social sciences, and many more.

One of the greatest things about research is its diversity -- it gives you the opportunity to further your education by contributing to something that you're passionate about. You have the ability to learn something new, whether it relates to academics or discovering something new about yourself.

The opportunities available at MRU are no different in offering students chances to embark on research across a variety of fields. Whether it involves investigating ways to revive rural economies in Alberta or digging through the National Archives in Ottawa, Mount Royal has a number of opportunities to ensure students from all faculties can be involved in research.

How Do I Get Involved?

The first thing you need to do as a student looking to get involved in research is decide what interests you and then pursue it. Talk to people from different departments: professors, teaching assistants and even peers that have previous experience in undergraduate research.

Before applying for any research opportunities, make sure you have an up-to-date resume and show enthusiasm throughout the application process.
