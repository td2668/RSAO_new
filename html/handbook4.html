4.0 Internal Sources

The only regular internal source is CUEF scholarships for student 'learn while you research' assistants.

CUEF Scholarships

The CUEF is a unique opportunity to directly fund undergraduate student research in any field. The focus is on student research learning, not on the faculty project (of course, one facilitates the other). There is a maximum of $5,000 available for each Student Research Scholarship. Although there is no stipulation of 'one per faculty member', the need to distribute funds across disciplines means that it is unlikely that a faculty member would host two CUEF students at the same time.

Up to 20 of these scholarships are distributed per year, with two intake periods:

  1. September call, mid-October deadline for Fall/Winter semester
  2. February call, mid-March due date for Summer semester

The application comes from the student, but is developed in close consultation with a faculty supervisor. This could be driven either way: a student with an interesting project idea may approach a potential supervisor; or a faculty member with a project might locate a suitable, interested student. The project should be of suitable length to fit within a (max) 12 weeks period, and must result in specific learning outcomes. If the research work is a portion of a larger project you must ensure that it stands alone to some degree.

Steps Involved:

  1. Identify your student / identify your supervisor;
  2. Develop a project proposal. It must focus on student learning outcomes. (500 words)
  3. Complete ethical review if required;
  4. Complete a budget (80% or more must go to the student directly);
  5. Faculty member completes a course outline. For the duration of the scholarship the student is registered in a research-learning course.
  6. Faculty member completes an RESL Course Approval form.
  7. Faculty member writes a recommendation; student submits a transcript;
  8. Chair and Dean sign, and all documentation is submitted to Research Services.
