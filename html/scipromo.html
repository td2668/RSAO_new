Darwin, Natural Selection and SEX:150 Years of Getting It On

With Jay Ingram, host of Daily Planet, and special musical guests

Mature content — not suitable for young children

Thursday, February 12 at 7 p.m.
Ross Glen Hall, Centre for Continuous Learning - Mount Royal College
Tickets at the door for $10 or $5 with any student ID (Proceeds to a Mount Royal Bachelor of Science student scholarship)

Everyone knows that Charles Darwin transformed our sense of what it means to be human by showing that we evolved from non-human ancestors. But most don't realize how far-reaching his ideas are. Much of modern dating and mating behaviour — from pick-up lines in a singles bar to long-term mate selection — can be shown to be the result of unconscious drives shaped by tens of thousands of years of evolution.

About Jay Ingram: Jay Ingram has been the host of the award-winning science show Daily Planet on Discovery Channel Canada since it began in 1995. Prior to joining Discovery, Jay hosted CBC radio’s national science show, Quirks and Quarks, from 1979 to 1992. He is a Distinguished Alumnus of the University of Alberta and has received four honorary doctorates. Jay has also written 10 books, including the recently released The Daily Planet Book of Cool Ideas: Global Warming and What People Are Doing about It, published by Penguin Canada.
