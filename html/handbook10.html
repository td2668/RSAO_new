10.0 Financial Administration

First Steps After Receiving a Grant (external, generic)

Notification: Most federal granting agencies notify the ORS about your success (or lack thereof) before notifying you. Once you have the news, the details will typically arrive by mail within 2-4 weeks. If you were denied, you will receive comments from the committees that will hopefully enable you to be more successful on reapplication. With over-subscribed agencies such as the CIHR this first-time-denial, fix, reapply scenario is considered quite typical.

Account Setup: A Notice of Award (and soon after a cheque) will arrive in the ORS and in the Finance office at about the same time as the details (or be delayed until the granting period begins). Finance will then contact you about setting up a grant account.

Ethical Approval: If you require ethical approval for your work, this must have already occurred, or your grant funds will be held until approval is verified.

Budget: Finance will require that you submit a budget to them, using standardized budget codes. Your yearly financial statements to the granting council will be based on these budget divisions, so if you are working with a finicky council or a grant with fixed categorical expenditures, make sure you code your expenses properly.

First Steps (internal or institutional grant such as SIG)

Notification: You will first be notified of success/failure by the ORS or by the committee responsible for the grant (typically the Scholarly Activities Committee).

Ethical Approval: As with external grants, you will be required to obtain a certificate of approval from the appropriate Ethics Committee if your work involves human or animal subjects (or falls within the parameters of 'requiring ethical approval' as defined in the TRU Policy ED-15-2 Section 2) before your funds can be released. Note: It is your responsibility to flag this requirement and to seek and obtain approval. TRU has signed an agreement that makes all federal funds dependent on our performing appropriate ethical reviews of all relevant research. If you don't flag such research you put everyone else's funding at risk.

Account Setup - SAC Grants: Your grant will receive a unique grant number that should be used in all requests and correspondence. You currently will not be granted access to your account via FAST (TRU web-based account management), as SAC accounts are held in a different database. Questions regarding balances and spending authorizations should be addressed to the Research Finance Officer, who is specifically responsible for these accounts.

Account Setup - Other Institutional Grants: These are individual or group grants awarded to the institution and (typically) distributed by SAC using grant-specific criteria. These grants function in a similar manner to standard external grants, except that TRU is responsible for reminders, reporting, renewals, etc.

Spending (all grants)

What You Can/Can't Sign For: You have signing authority on the grant account assigned to you. If it is an external grant the account is in your department's business unit. Any expenditures above $1000 (or whatever limit your Chair/Dean authorizes when the account is set up) require a Chair/Dean's signature. Purchases of Capital Items (assets over $1000) will also go the the VP Finance for approval. Also, any cash expenditures, reimbursements, or travel advances/claims require a Chair/Dean's signature.
Institutional grants (i.e. those distributed by TRU) fall into the Research Office's business unit. The Research Coordinator (Grants Officer) in the ORS can sign for large, cash, or travel expenditures.

Buying Things: Items < $100 can be purchased for cash and reimbursed with a petty cash form (must be signed, then you take it to the cashier in Finance). You can also use a Purchase Requisition. A PR must be used for items over $100. This is the only form you will need. If you are buying from another department (this includes the Bookstore) you use an Interdepartmental Invoice.

Travel: No matter what the source of the grant funds your travel expenditures must abide by TRU travel policy. See the Finance travel pages, and note the flat rates for travel using your automobile to certain common destinations. Travel advances require signatures on a Travel Advance Form as noted above, and if your final cost is > 110% of your estimate a further signature is required when you submit your Expense Report or your Within Region Expense Report.

Contracts: TRU uses a standard Professional Services Agreement (p1 and p2), which must be filled out in addition to any contracts between you and a contractor. These require several signatures and take a bit of time to set up. However, they streamline multiple invoice payments. Just quote the PSA number on all invoices. Read this synopsis and contact Gloria Rowe at 5604 for more details.

Hiring: If you hire research assistants or others you are required to work through the Human Resources office and to abide by all Faculty Association and CUPE agreements. This typically requires some time and effort, and will take you up into the >$20/hr range including benefits.
Consider alternatives before taking the step of hiring through HR. Is this a student learning situation? If you plan your project around students learning while researching rather than using hired researchers, you can take advantage of scholarships such as the CUEF. See the ORS for details.
There are also arrangements in place to allow the hiring of post-doctoral researchers at standard post-doc rates.
Also consider hiring your research expertise by contracting an external agency.

Note: before taking any of the above steps, do ensure that you are aware of the proper definition of 'Research Learning' or the restrictions on who comprises a valid 'contractor'.
