2.0 The Grant Process at MRU

Research Services processes all external grant applications for MRU. The signature of the Director of the ORS is the "University Authorized Signature" for TRU, and is required on all external applications. The ORS is also available to review your application, however the following schedule must be adhered to.

Due Prior to Deadline Review Type Final Versions Due
10 working days Full review 2 working days prior
5 working days Administrative review 2 working days prior
Less than 5 working days Basic check and signature 2 working days prior

Although we will make every effort, the ORS cannot guarantee that an application will be sent on time if submitted less than 2 days prior to the agency deadline. This is due to workload scheduling, the need to verify RTS requests, etc.

Note that 'Agency Deadline' must be back-timed to include any transport delays (where the grant is due at the agency on the deadline). Most couriers do not guarantee overnight to Ottawa from Kamloops, so 2 days should be allowed.

Steps in the Submission Process
  1. Contact the ORS Director via email during grant development to discuss requirements and issues specific to the type of grant;
  2. The researcher fills out an ORS Grant Form online. This form requires the digital signature of yourself and your Dean, and should be completed well in advance of the ORS deadline. The agency form may also require physical signatures.
  3. Deliver the completed application, with all required signatures, to the ORS. The preferred method is to send/drop the signature pages and deliver the remainder via email (agency PDFs AND your source documents to permit in-line commenting).
    Physical delivery can either be directly to the Director's office (House 6/7 - Research Administration) or to the reception desk in the Advanced Technology Centre (Trades 155 - staffed 8:30 - 4:30).
  4. The grant is reviewed as follows (or as requested, time permitting):

    Basic Check: Status of all faculty verified; RTS or other university contribution verified.

    Administrative Review: Above + Check on all budget items for consistency and adherence to policy; Review of 'Research Summary' and other non-technical sections for readability by general audience.

    Full Review: Above + Full reading and analysis of budget justification; Detailed comments on non-technical sections.

  5. The researcher is contacted via email with suggestions and comments.
  6. Corrections/additions are applied by the researcher and the full number of required documents are brought to the ORS. If any changes that affect the signature page (e.g. budget total changes) then new signatures are required from your Dean and or Chair.

    Digital-only Submissions (e.g. NSERC): All of the above deadlines remain in effect. However, 'delivery' is via web submission. Do NOT depend on the agency to inform the ORS of your submission. Send an email.

  7. The application is assembled and signed by the Director of the ORS. The Director's signature is the "Authorized University Signature" and acts as the Presidential Designate signature when one is required. This signature has different meanings for each agency, but typically indicates that the applicant is a faculty member at the institution and that the institution guarantees any listed contributions.
  8. The application is couriered to the agency before the deadline date.
