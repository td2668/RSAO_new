9.0 Compliance - Ethics

Any research work involving human or animal subjects must be approved by the appropriate committee before research funding can be released. This means that you can apply for funding, but TRU will withhold any funds awarded until you satisfy the requirements. The tri-councils (NSERC, SSHRC, CIHR) leave this authorization procedure in TRU's hands. This means that we are required to use a methodology approved by Ottawa, which may appear rather extensive, particularly for small projects.

Human Subjects

The Research Ethics Human Subjects Committee has endeavoured to streamline the approval process by appointing small sub-committees that vet all straightforward applications. You can apply at any time, however as you might guess, summer applications will likely take longer due to intermittent availability of members.

Apply When: Anytime, though to reach the full committee you should submit by the first Friday of the month
Apply Where: Using the web-based system.
Period: 1 Year - (submit annual status report for renewal)
5 Years - Maximum before reapplication is necessary
Forms and Instructions:

Manual forms are still required for Student Projects, Project Ammendments, and Annual Status Reports.
