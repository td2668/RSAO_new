Human Ethics

Mount Royal University maintains a Human Research Ethics Board (HREB) that is compliant with the Tri-Council Policy Statement (TCPS) on human research ethics.  You can find out more about the TCPS at http://pre.ethics.gc.ca/eng/.

Mount Royal's HREB

  • reviews all human behavioural research activities that either originate at MRU and/or that take place at MRU
  • is not certified to review research studies that fall under the Health Information Act (HIA), that is, studies that either access or create patient records. These studies may be reviewed by CHREB at the University of Calgary (see below)
  • does not review biomedical studies that involve biomedical treatments or interventions.

In terms of the general ethics review process at MRU

  • you need to secure ethics clearance for your study before you begin to recruit and/or gather data from your participants
  • clearance generally takes between 4 and 6 weeks, depending on the complexity and risk inherent in your study
  • once you have ethics clearance, you also need to seek further clearance from HREB if you wish to make any changes to your processes, procedures or documents, during the study
  • you must also follow all appropriate legislation, procedures, laws and policies that apply to your study from granting bodies, the institution and/or provincial or federal governments
  • HREB accepts ethics applications at any time (but does not process them during June-Aug)
  • HREB works with you to secure ethics clearance for your study
  • To help facilitate a relatively speedy review, it is very important that you submit your application well before you want/need it cleared (at least 4 weeks beforehand) and that you hand in an application that is clear, concise and comprehensive

The Human Research Ethics Review Process

To help you prepare for and navigate through the ethics review process, we have prepared a guide that you should read.  This guide covers more than just the ethics review as it also includes academic integrity and risk management issues.  It shows you how the human research ethics review fits into the overall “research ethics” process at MRU.  Follow the path through the document that matches your specific needs.

The Human Research Ethics Application

Once you have read through the process and are ready to submit an ethics application (if that is what you should do), download the application below.   Please note that this document is interactive i.e. depending on the choices you make new questions may pop up.  As well, there is contextual help in most sections.  Please read the contextual help if it applies to you, and send us feedback (to hreb@mtroyal.ca) on whether you found it helpful and/or whether other information would also be of help.  If you have any questions at all, do not hesitate to contact either Glynis McCance (403-440-6069) in the Office of Research Services or the current Chair of the HREB.  They would be happy to assist you.  The other members of the HREB can also be approached for assistance.
