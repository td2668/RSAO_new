Research and Scholarship Advisory Committee

The RSAC, a subcommittee of General Faculties Council, is a broadly representative body of faculty and staff that:
  • Acts in an advisory capacity to the AVP Research
  • Reviews the institution's Strategic Research Plan annually
  • Develops, considers and reviews research-related policies and procedures
  • Provides an internal communication mechanism between Faculty Councils and Research Services
  • Considers applications for 'Research Centres'

Responsible Conduct of Research Committee

The Responsible Conduct of Research Committee works to develop and implement educational programs about research integrity for both staff and students. They also evaluate research-related conflicts of interest and, in the rare case of actual allegations of misconduct, they provide a first fine of evaluation before involving a formal tribunal. The RCRC has a member from every faculty, and meets regularly to discuss its educational responsibilities.

Undergraduate Research Standing Committee

As an undergraduate university with a mission to integrate research with teaching wherever possible, Mount Royal places a heavy focus on student involvement in research activity -- both student and faculty-driven. The UGR Standing Committee, sponsored through the office of the AVP Research, is the driving force behind this initiative. The committee meets to discuss and plan celebrations, awards, curriculum integration and promotional activities.

The committee members are only the tip of the iceberg for this activity. Join our Blackboard discussion group by contacting the Chair (below).

Scholarship Review Committee (Internal Grants)

The Scholarship Review Committee (SRC) reviews all institution-wide internal grant applications, makes decisions on ranking, and provides recommendations on funding to the AVP Research. The SRC also acts as a team of topic-specialist reviewers that may be called upon by the Grants Facilitator, the Human Research Ethics Board, or individual faculty (through Research Services), to review specific portions of applications. Committee members may also be called upon to review specific types of external calls where the institution is the manager of the funds, such as through AACTI or SSHRC Institutional Grants.

Institutes Review Committee

This committee's mission is to:
  1. review existing and proposed Institutes and recommend establishment, continuation or revocation.
  2. review recommendations for appointments of Directors of Institutes or revocations of that appointment.
  3. review recommendations for members of each Institute's Advisory Committee or revocation of that appointment.
