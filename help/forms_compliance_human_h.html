Human Subjects - Health

Research Involving Human Participants (Health) is defined as an undertaking intended to extend the collective knowledge of a field through a disciplinary inquiry or systematic investigation, which involves living individuals, human remains, cadavers, tissues, biological fluids, embryos or foetuses. It involves surgery, clinical interventions such as the administration or testing of drugs, medical devices, medical imaging or diagnostic techniques, taking of blood or other specimens, exercise programs, and/or the analysis of clinical data (e.g. laboratory, physiological, kinesiological or biological) obtained from physical interventions or medical records or studies of a clinical nature involving the linkage of data from existing databases.
